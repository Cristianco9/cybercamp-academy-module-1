# Concepts Before Moving Forward

You are probably excited to begin learning about each of the areas you have just 
discovered! However, before continuing, let's do a quick review of some related 
concepts.

---

## Cybersecurity
Cybersecurity refers to everything involved in protecting electronic data from 
unauthorized access or use.

---

## Information Security
Information security consists of the measures taken to safeguard and protect 
information while maintaining the **confidentiality, availability, and integrity** 
of data.

---

## Secure Coding
Secure coding is an area of cybersecurity dedicated to ensuring that software 
development follows cybersecurity functional requirements and relevant standards 
depending on the use case.

---

## Application Security
Within this area you will find **penetration testing (pentesting)**, which focuses 
on performing penetration tests and vulnerability analysis.

This means ethically and legally simulating a **hack or cyberattack** on a web 
application, mobile application, or network in order to identify vulnerabilities, 
report them to developers or system administrators, and protect the system from 
real-world attacks.

---

## Cryptography
Cryptography is the field that studies the encryption of information. Its goal 
is to ensure that a message can only be read by authorized individuals.

---

## Steganography
Steganography is a technique that involves embedding a secret message within 
information that appears harmless or ordinary.

---

## Computer Networks
Computer networking is the area of computer science that studies communication 
and interconnection between two or more computers through specific technologies.

---

## Social Engineering
Social engineering consists of manipulation techniques designed to obtain 
sensitive information, system access, or permissions for an attacker.

This manipulation is performed on legitimate users without them realizing it.  
For example, a bank employee may be tricked into revealing confidential 
information.

---

## Cyber Intelligence
Cyber intelligence involves the acquisition and analysis of information to 
identify, track, and predict cyber capabilities, intentions, and activities that 
help detect 
potential threats.

---

## Digital Forensic Engineering
Digital forensic engineering focuses on identifying the cause of failures or 
incidents in a system, either to improve the system or to find those responsible 
for the attack.

---

## Scripting
Scripting is a specialized area that focuses on automating tasks and hacking 
tools such as exploits, helping facilitate cybersecurity audits and vulnerability 
discovery.

---

## Malware
Malware simply means **Malicious Software**. It refers to software designed to 
obtain, alter, or damage digital information or systems.

There are several types of malware:

### Spyware
A type of malware that secretly monitors activity on an infected device and may 
even control it. Typically, it is operated by an external attacker using a system 
known as **Command and Control (C2)**.

### Virus
Any piece of code capable of infecting your computer and spreading to other 
systems.

### Trojan
A type of malware that appears to perform a legitimate function while secretly 
carrying malicious intentions.

### Ransomware
Software that hijacks a computer by taking control of it until a ransom payment 
is made.

### Rootkits
Malware designed to gain administrative privileges by attempting to reach and 
control the **kernel**, the core component of an operating system.
